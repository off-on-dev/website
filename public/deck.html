About OffOn

For the People Who Keep Open Source Alive

→ Sustain the people behind open source and grow the maintainers of tomorrow
→ Everyone belongs here: learners, engineers, contributors, maintainers, writers, designers, translators, organisers
→ Learn through real challenges, share what you know, help the next generation find their footing
→ Community knowledge that is preserved, passed on, and built to grow

always on. always open. always learning.

  • OffOn exists to sustain the people behind open source and to develop the maintainers of tomorrow. Most platforms and foundations are built with enterprises and legal teams in mind. OffOn is built for the individuals who actually write the code, review the pull requests, and keep projects alive day to day.
  • The community is open to everyone who cares about open source, whether you write code or not. Developers, SREs, writers, designers, translators, organisers, OSPO members, first-time contributors, and long-time maintainers all belong here. If you are anywhere on that journey, from curious about open source to running a project with thousands of users, this space is for you.
  • The way OffOn works is through hands-on challenges built around real tools and real scenarios. Not tutorials, not marketing content. Things you can apply, fork, and build on.
  • And one of the things we care most about is making sure knowledge does not disappear. Chat threads lose context the moment they scroll away. OffOn is designed to structure and preserve what the community learns, so it can be passed on to the next person who needs it.

Speaking

What Makes a Good Talk

→ An intro to the open source project you maintain or contribute to
→ A demo of something you built with open source tools, including what broke
→ A concept explained clearly for a mixed audience, with a working example
→ A contribution story: your first PR, a tricky bug, a community experience

Not a good fit: product demos, vendor pitches, or talks that require a commercial tool to be relevant.
