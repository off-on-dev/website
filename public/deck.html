Speaking

What makes a good talk

→ An intro to the open source project you maintain or contribute to
→ A demo of something you built with open source tools, including what broke
→ A concept explained clearly for a mixed audience, with a working example
→ A contribution story: your first PR, a tricky bug, a community experience

Not a good fit: product demos, vendor pitches, or talks that require a commercial tool to be relevant.

About OffOn

For the people who keep open source alive

→ A vendor-neutral, community-driven space built around one goal: sustaining the people behind open source and creating the maintainers of tomorrow
→ From curious learners to first-time contributors to long-term maintainers, regardless of background or project
→ Open to everyone: developers, writers, designers, translators, organisers. Sponsors fund the work, they do not shape the mission

always on. always open. always learning.

- OffOn is the organisation behind this event. We exist to sustain the people behind open source, not the projects. The people. - Most platforms built around open source are designed for enterprises, foundations, or marketing teams. OffOn is built for the individuals who actually do the work. - Vendor-neutral by design: any sponsor funds the work, not the mission. Dynatrace is the founding sponsor but does not control what OffOn builds or says. - Open to everyone: not just developers. Writers, designers, translators, event organisers. All count. - The figure in the corner is Nyx, the OffOn mascot. Use it as a visual anchor, not a talking point.

Our Founding Sponsor

Why Dynatrace cares

→ Dynatrace builds on open source, contributes to it, and depends on its long-term health. Supporting the ecosystem is part of how they operate, not a side activity.
→ Most corporate OSS investment flows toward foundations and large strategic projects. Far less of it reaches the individuals who write the code and keep projects alive day to day.
→ Sponsoring OffOn is about being a responsible participant in the ecosystem they all rely on. Not a marketing exercise.
